Odoacer deposes Romulus, Court in Ravenna de facto dissolved.

By: Marcus Tullius, 5 September 1229 AVC

As of yesterday, and with Senate support, the barbarian Odoacer has forced the Western emperor Romulus Augustulus to vacate his position and surrender his crown.

In the wake of shift in power, Odoacer has declared himself King of the Herules, effectively marking the end of the Imperial Court in Ravenna. Reports say the barbarian king spared the young emperor Romulus's life and exiled him, unlike his father Orestes who was executed last August.

As the court in Ravenna is extinct, the Eastern Emperor Zeno still maintains that Julius Nepos is the Western Emperor, viewing the boy emperor Romulus as a usurper.As for Eastern opinion on Odoacer's rule, the East are to recognize the barbarian king as "Patrician of Italy;" administering the region for the empire while Nepos is recognized as regnant.

Though the imperial court has vanished, the Senate is still expected to carry on while under Odoacer's rule. Their cooperation with each other and the Eastern court in Constantinople is expected to carry on as the empire navigates "turbulent times."

This article will be updated as the aftermath of the coup is fully realized over the coming days.
